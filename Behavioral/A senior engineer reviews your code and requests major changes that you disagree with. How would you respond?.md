## What Interviewers Want

- Open-mindedness
- Professional communication
- Respect for code reviews
- Ability to discuss trade-offs
- Focus on the best technical solution

---

## Good Approach

1. Listen carefully to the feedback.
2. Ask questions to understand the reasoning.
3. Explain your own approach respectfully.
4. Discuss the trade-offs.
5. Choose the solution that best benefits the project.

---

## Avoid

- Becoming defensive.
- Arguing emotionally.
- Treating code reviews as personal criticism.

---

## 45-Second Interview Answer

> I would first understand why the reviewer suggested the changes instead of assuming my solution is better. If I believe my approach has advantages, I'd explain the trade-offs respectfully and discuss them with the reviewer. Ultimately, my goal is to choose the best solution for the project, even if that means changing my original implementation.
