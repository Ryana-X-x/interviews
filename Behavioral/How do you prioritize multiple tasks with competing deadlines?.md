## What the Interviewer is Evaluating

- Time Management
- Prioritization
- Communication
- Decision Making
- Adaptability

---

## Good Answer Structure

### 1. Assess Urgency and Impact

Identify:

- Which tasks are business-critical?
- Which deadlines are fixed?
- Which tasks block other people?

Start with the highest-impact work.

---

### 2. Break Work into Milestones

Large tasks become smaller, manageable pieces.

Example:

```
Feature

↓

API

↓

Database

↓

Testing

↓

Deployment
```

This makes progress easier to track.

---

### 3. Communicate Risks Early

If deadlines conflict:

- Inform your manager early.
- Explain the trade-offs.
- Ask for clarification if priorities are unclear.

Never wait until the deadline to report delays.

---

### 4. Reprioritize When Needed

If new high-priority work arrives:

- Re-evaluate existing tasks.
- Adjust the plan.
- Confirm the new priorities with stakeholders.

---

### 5. Keep Stakeholders Updated

Provide regular progress updates.

Example:

```
Completed API.

Currently testing.

Deployment scheduled for tomorrow.
```

Clear communication builds trust and avoids surprises.

## Tips

✅ Prioritize based on **business impact**, not just who asks first.

✅ Communicate delays as soon as they're identified.

✅ Be flexible when priorities change.

❌ Don't try to do everything at once.

❌ Don't stay silent if you're likely to miss a deadline.

---

## 60-Second Interview Answer

> When I have multiple competing deadlines, I first assess each task based on its urgency, business impact, and dependencies. I break larger tasks into smaller milestones so I can track progress effectively. If I identify any risks or potential delays, I communicate them early instead of waiting until the deadline. As priorities change, I reassess my plan, discuss trade-offs with stakeholders when necessary, and keep everyone updated on my progress. This helps ensure that the most important work is delivered first while maintaining clear communication throughout the process.
