## Best Structure (STAR Method)

### Situation

What was the project or requirement?

---

### Task

What challenge did you need to solve?

---

### Action

- How did you learn?
- Documentation?
- Tutorials?
- Building a small prototype?
- Asking teammates?

---

### Result

What was the outcome?

- Completed the task.
- Improved performance.
- Delivered on time.

---

### Reflection

Mention what you learned and what you'd improve next time.

---

## Interview Keywords

- Self-learning
- Adaptability
- Problem Solving
- Continuous Learning
- Ownership

---

## 60-Second Answer (Example)

> During one of my projects, I needed to build a backend using Spring Boot, but I had limited experience with it. I first learned the fundamentals through the official documentation and a structured course, then built small prototype applications to understand concepts like dependency injection and REST APIs. After that, I applied what I learned to the actual project, successfully delivering the required functionality on time. That experience improved my ability to learn new technologies quickly, and it reinforced the value of building small hands-on projects instead of only reading documentation.
